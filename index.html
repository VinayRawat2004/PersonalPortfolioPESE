PESE Classes Highlights

Session 1: Introduction

Got an introduction to the subject and to ma'am. Ma'am introduced herself. Did a fun activity of translating Hindi song lyrics into English. Talked about why we hesitate to talk about ourselves. Discussed the importance of being energetic, disciplined, and using positive words while speaking.

Session 2: Skills, SMART Goals & CV Basics

Learned about different types of skills and the SMART goal framework. SMART = Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Practiced Time Jar activity to understand task prioritization. Learned about basic CV structure and sections. Weekly quote-learning task (3–5 quotes) was introduced.

Session 3: SWOT & Perspective

Learned about SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats). Discussed the importance of certifications. Did a perspective-building activity. Practiced short meditation to improve clarity and focus.

Session 4: Interview Flow & Self-Introduction

Learned how interviews are structured and what to expect. Formed lab groups. Practiced how to introduce ourselves properly with real examples. Ma'am shared stories to explain concepts clearly.

Session 5: Roleplay & Extempore

Participated in roleplay activities and practiced extempore speeches. Topics included empathy and space tourism. Ma'am gave tasks quickly and directly. The session was short and active.

Session 6: Group Discussion & Animal Analogy

Did GD on topics like “Is the US still a superpower?” and “Civic Sense in India.” Learned how to speak, listen, and cooperate in group discussions. Ma'am shared the story of the monk and mirror to teach self-awareness.

Animal Analogy in GD:
🦁 Lion – Starts strong and leads the group
🐘 Elephant – Wise, calm, and supportive
🦊 Fox – Thinks quickly, finds smart openings, and makes sharp points
🦜 Parrot – Repeats what others say
🐱 Cat – Quiet and avoids speaking

We learned it's best to be like a lion or a calm elephant in GDs.

Session 7: Interview Practice

Practiced answering common interview questions. Each student was asked to answer in front of the class. Ma’am guided us on how to give structured and confident answers with real examples.

Session 8: Behavioural Questions

Focused on behavioural and situational questions used in interviews. Answered based on real-life experiences to show thinking, values, and problem-solving ability.
